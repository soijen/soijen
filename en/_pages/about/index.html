OUR STORY

About Sóijen

At Sóijen, we are first and foremost outdoor adventurers with a passion for the wind-swept peaks and icy fjords of southern Patagonia. We create designs that inspire fellow adventurers to get out and explore off-the-beaten-path areas in Patagonia and beyond.

Our products are made with recycled materials and partnerships with environmentally-conscious vendors. We support and collaborate with local agencies and environmental initiatives to preserve and protect this one-of-a-kind region.

What does Sóijen mean?

We drew inspiration for our name from the Selk’nam – the indigenous people of southern Patagonia. The Selk’nam were nomadic hunters, traveling by foot across the desolate landscapes at the end of the world. When they crossed paths, they celebrated with unique footraces called káiyik, sprinting around lakes or racing to the tops of hills. The winner was the sóijen – or champion runner.

Sustainability & Materials

At Sóijen we are committed to sustainability. We collaborate with environmentally conscious suppliers and maximize the recycled content in all our product materials and packaging. At least 1% of each purchase is donated to environmental non-profits and initiatives in Chile.

Our tote bags and pouches

The fabric for our tote bags is a blend of 65% recycled cotton and 35% recycled post consumer poly. The recycled cotton is recuperated from fabric waste generated during the manufacturing process of new clothes, traditionally sent to landfills. The recycled poly is derived from 100% recycled PET plastic bottles. All our tote bags are made in USA.

Our posters & paper products

All of our posters and paper products are printed on paper that maximizes recycled content and post-consumer waste, and with Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) certification and/or Green Seal certification.

Packaging

The majority of our packaging is plastic-free, and consists of recycled kraft paper or other recycled paper products. Our art prints are protected by an Eco Clear plant-based PLA sleeve that is certified compostable.

Get Inspired. Go Outside. & Give Back.

Support the wild places you love. Each year we donate a percentage of our profits to support environmental initiatives in Chile. In 2018 and 2019 we partnered with Fundación Reforestemos, a non profit dedicated to recovering degraded ecosystems in Patagonia and other areas in Chile.

Donations are used to plant native trees in regions with high ecological value throughout Patagonia and Chile. For more information about Fundación Reforestemos and their mission, or to make a direct donation, check out their website.
